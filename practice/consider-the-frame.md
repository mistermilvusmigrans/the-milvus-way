<h1>Consider the Frame</h1>

---

# Key Idea 1
An idea is never grasped without a frame, i.e.:

<details>
<summary>Context</summary>
<p>
The set of facts/ideas in relation to which the idea is being grasped
</p>
</details>

<details>
<summary>Purpose(s)</summary>
<p>
The value(s) whose pursuit shapes what is considered and how.
</p>
</details>

Context includes (but is not limited to):

- Philosophical outlook
- Psychological aspects (long-term and situational)
- Practices and habits

# Key Idea 2
A frame consists of ideas and their relationships.

=> It is not above the consideration we give ideas.

# Practice
- Identify the frame I am operating within
- Make it as operative to my thinking as it deserves to be

# Example
**1.**

When considering higher rates of divorce, instead of treating this as a sign of worsening relationship strength/quality (thereby operating within the frame that commitment and "loyalty" to a bond is the primary element of a relationship's strength/quality), consider: what is a relationship for, in the end? Is the model of relationship applicable to humans in general, or only to certain humans in certain circumstances? What about commitment is inherently good, if anything, and what about breaking off a commitment is inherently bad, if anything?

Because higher rates of divorce may well be signs of improvement in the human condition: more empowerment to pursue one's judgement (especially for those previously not as empowered, e.g. women in some societies), less tolerance of poor or even harmful partnerships, etc.

---

**2.**

When considering what a criminal should face for a violent crime, consider whether retribution is aligned with moral values and valuable goals for humans beings. Vengeance may feel good, but is this feeling a valid argument for vengeance, or simply an urge that demands satisfaction? What if punishment is not based on moral reasoning but on emotional reaction? What does the data show about the effects of punishment? What are the other ways violent crimes can be addressed, perhaps more effectively?

> **Reference**: [*The Lie Everyone Believes*, @DarkMatter2525](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7o3ukHKJn6w)

---

**3.**

What if beauty is a vague value-statement disguised as an objective/intrinsic quality, serving to assign neutral traits to a hierarchy of values, ascribing inherent moral qualities to something that is morally neutral? What if its use can turn judgement from appreciation to discrimination?
