# Statement
Worth, for a self, is the objective standing of the systems that compose it, together with the quality of integration between them - not a subjective wish, but a recognition of real potential for health, cognitive-psychological capacity, and agency, where agency is the successful integration of the other systems into a single capacity to act on one's own judgement, rather than a third system standing alongside them. This is the self-facing counterpart to [life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md](./life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md): where that entry establishes that truth-orientation is the form life-affirmation takes for a volitional being, this entry gives content to what recognising that life-affirmation's own standing potential actually consists in.

# Derivation
Source essay 1 states the triad directly: *"your life is worth living if you see a potential for health, mental capacity (cognitively and psychologically) and agency; the more the potential for each, the more the 'worth.'"* It is explicit that this is not a matter of preference: *"'worth' in this context should not represent a subjective wish or desire but an objective judgement of the potential of our existence."*

**Mapping the triad onto systems, rather than treating it as an unstructured list.** [the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md](./the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md) establishes that a self is composed of systems with genuinely distinct natures, each with its own point of failure, and distinguishes failure *within* a system from failure *of integration between* systems that are each individually sound. Read against this, worth's triad is not three parallel items - it is two systems plus a measure of the integration between them. Health tracks the standing of the biological system. Cognitive-psychological capacity tracks the standing of the cognitive/psychological system. Agency is not a third system alongside these - it is what successful integration between them consists in: the capacity to actually act on one's own judgement, which is exactly what fails, per the [the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md](./the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md)'s second failure type, when individually sound systems are not properly coupled. "Freedom" (source essay 2), read independently, confirms this without deriving it from that entry at all: freedom is defined there as *"the capacity of an individual to act on their own judgement, moral and/or practical"* - a capacity of the integrated whole acting as one, not a property any single system holds in isolation.

**Giving leverage's allocation a target, for the first time.** [leverage-governs-allocation.md](./leverage-governs-allocation.md) has an open edge stating that "leverage" smuggles in a judgement of importance it never derives - it names essentials as context-and-purpose-dependent but does not say what the purpose actually is, for a self. "The Chains of the Pursuit of Happiness" (source essay 3) supplies exactly this, independently of any leverage-language of its own: it argues against internalising a hierarchy of values aimed at a concrete, imagined state of happiness, and for internalising one aimed at *"wide-ranging capacity,"* because such capacity is *"particular in acquisition... while being generalisable in application,"* whereas a concrete end-state "can obfuscate the version of our values we can actually achieve in our context." "Generalisable in application" is a description of leverage, arrived at from a different essay, about a different topic, without the term itself being used. This gives leverage's allocation its missing target: what is worth allocating attention and effort toward, at the level of a self, is exactly the capacity that generalises, i.e. the standing of health, cognitive-psychological capacity, and the integration (agency) between them, rather than pursuit of any particular imagined end-state.

**This independently reinforces the argument against happiness-focus.** "The Chains of the Pursuit of Happiness" (source essay 3) arrives at a closely related conclusion as "Freedom" (source essay 2) from an entirely different angle - not by testing whether happiness equals self-preservation, but by arguing directly that happiness-focus, as a concrete motivational target, is unreliable and potentially disempowering compared to capacity-focus. This is worth noting explicitly: the two essays are not in tension, and the earlier decision to move away from happiness-focus is not backtracked but reinforced by this entry.

**Felt states as partial evidence, diagnosed rather than taken at face value.** source essay 1's own supporting observations - a felt sense of efficacy or apathy, confidence or overwhelm - are offered as evidence for worth's standing, not as the standard of worth itself, consistent with [felt-states-as-data-and-register.md](./felt-states-as-data-and-register.md). That entry's diagnostic (whether a felt state resolves to re-examination, to time/physiology, or to neither) is what is actually needed to tell which of the three components a given felt signal of high or low worth is reporting on, rather than reading a felt state as a direct, unmediated report of worth in general.

**Cross-reference back to the [life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md](./life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md)'s surviving open edge, stated here rather than there.** That entry left open how to tell whether an action risking continued existence for the sake of correspondence is life-affirmation carried to its conclusion or self-undermining failure wearing that justification. Worth supplies the missing instrument for that test, without needing the that entry itself to be revised: an action that risks or ends bare continuation while preserving or advancing the worth that continuation was for - cognitive-psychological capacity and agency specifically - is the floor being honoured, not violated, which is exactly source essay 1's own conclusion: *"only that which makes life worth living can be worth dying for."* An action that degrades worth's components with no compensating preservation is the self-undermining case. This resolves the open edge's testability problem without altering the terminus entry's own text; the cross-reference is recorded here.

# Domain of validity
Scoped as the rest of this section is scoped - a volitional, conceptual consciousness of the kind established in [action-as-total-expression.md](./action-as-total-expression.md) and [the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md](./the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md). This entry is explicitly self-facing: it is a standard for the standing of a particular self's own systems, distinct from (though continuous with) [life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md](./life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md)'s claim about persistence as such.

# Self-application pass
**Checked against**: [the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md](./the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md): Does mapping worth's triad onto systems force a fixed, three-item taxonomy onto an entry that explicitly refuses a closed inventory? It should not be read this way: health and cognitive-psychological capacity are source essay 1's naming of two systems that entry's own illustrative list already gestures at (biological, cognitive/psychological), and agency is argued above to be integration-quality, not a third system. This entry does not claim these are the *only* systems a self has - only that these are the two source essay 1 names, plus the integration between them, and the taxonomy's openness is inherited, not closed off, by this entry.

**Checked against**: The [life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md](./life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md)'s "which life" open edge: Does treating health as a worth-component risk smuggling back bare biological survival, which that entry deliberately excluded? It should not, provided health is read as source essay 1 frames it throughout - health in service of *"a self-driven (i.e. volitional) survival,"* not mere biological survival on its own terms. This entry inherits that framing rather than loosening it, and should not be read as licensing a bare-survival reading of health in isolation from the other two components.

**Checked against**: The point of pursuing power/capacity-focus over happiness-focus from source essays 2 and 3: addressed directly in the "Derivation" above rather than left implicit - source essays 2 and 3 reinforce rather than reopen that decision.

**Checked against**: Whether "agency is integration, not a system" is asserted rather than tested: it is asserted here, on the strength of Freedom's independent definition and the systems entry's own failure-type distinction, but it has not been tested against a case where agency arguably fails for reasons that are not obviously integration-failures (e.g. a case where the integration is sound but the individual either lacks a system's basic functioning altogether, or lacks the standing to exercise judgement despite functioning systems and successful integration). This is named directly in "Open edges" rather than treated as settled.

# Open edges
- **Whether agency is fully explained as integration-quality.** The claim is stated and defended above but not stress-tested against edge cases - for instance, whether a case of agency failing under sound individual systems and sound integration between them (if such a case exists) would falsify the mapping or merely reveal a fourth element not yet named.
- **No method for comparing worth across components.** source essay 1 states "the more the potential for each, the more the "worth"" but gives no way to weigh a gain in one component against a loss in another - this will matter directly once the self-evaluation entry needs to render an actual verdict rather than simply diagnose which component is in play.
- **Trade-offs in conflict cases.** How worth's components should be weighed against each other when they genuinely conflict (e.g. risking health for a gain in cognitive-psychological capacity or agency) is closely related to the [life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md](./life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md)'s narrowed self-undermining-risk edge and is likely its own future working-result rather than something resolved here.
