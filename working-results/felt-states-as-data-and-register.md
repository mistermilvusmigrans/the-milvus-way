# Statement
Felt states are data about the standing of the self's various systems relative to what is - not themselves the standard by which worth or correctness is judged, and not a single undifferentiated stream but readouts whose origin must be tracked across the systems identified in [the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md](./the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md). But felt states are not only evidentiary. They are also the register in which correspondence or its absence, is actually lived - the felt form value-contact takes, not merely a signal pointing at something else.

> **KEY POINT**: These two roles do not compete: the objective standard governs whether a given register actually corresponds to the facts that it is potentially associated to, i.e. whether what accompanies felt states like accomplishment, apathy, or unease actually corresponds to those states, respectively.

# Derivation
[states-are-not-obstacles-they-are-data.md](../initialisation/states-are-not-obstacles-they-are-data.md) already established that sleep deprivation, hormones, fury and love are differently-tuned instruments to be read, not degradations to be managed or suppressed. This entry keeps that conclusion and gives it the architecture it was missing.

**Heterogeneity, explained rather than merely observed.** [the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md](./the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md) is what actually explains why states are differently-tuned in the first place: a felt state can be a readout from the biological system, from the cognitive/psychological system, or a signal that neither system alone produces, i.e. a report of failed integration between two systems each functioning correctly on their own terms. Treating felt states as one undifferentiated stream, to be read uniformly, would repeat the exact error that entry warned against: collapsing systems with genuinely distinct natures into a single channel.

**This resolves, rather than merely references, [the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md](./the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md)'s open edge on overlap with felt states.** Some felt states are biological-system readouts with no cognitive content to examine. Others are the apparatus itself surfacing a divergence report - the felt friction of an action pulling against a validated premise, or the discomfort of a premise nearing the edge of examination. These are not the same kind of signal, and the apparatus entry's open question - whether felt states and the apparatus describe one layer or two - is answered here: they are not two separate layers. Some felt states simply *are* the apparatus's own output, surfacing in experience; others originate elsewhere entirely. What was missing was not a merger of the two entries but a way to tell, in a given instance, which is which.

**The diagnostic, substantiated rather than asserted.** Take a concrete case: pre-presentation anxiety. This single, apparently uniform felt state could be reporting at least three structurally different things:

1. A pure biological-system signal (adrenaline/cortisol responding to a perceived-threat class of situation), carrying no examinable content;
2. An apparatus-level divergence report (per [the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md](./the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md)) - a genuine, examinable gap ("I have not actually prepared enough") surfacing as unease because there is a real premise underneath it worth examining;
3. A failure of integration, in the specific sense [the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md](./the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md) names as a distinct failure type - the cognitive judgment is sound ("I am prepared") and the biological arousal system is functioning correctly on its own terms, but the two are not coupled; confidence has not propagated into the body's alarm response.

These can feel nearly identical from the inside, which is the actual diagnostic problem: not "what does this feeling mean" as an abstract question, but "which of several structurally different things is this instance of a feeling." The distinction earns its keep only if it yields a workable test, not just a taxonomy, so the test is this: try the cheapest and least invasive intervention first, and let what does or does not resolve the state be the diagnostic.

- If honest re-examination of the underlying premise resolves the felt state once the check is done, that is evidence of case 2 - an apparatus report about a real gap, closed by closing the gap, exactly as [the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md](./the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md) already licenses.
- If re-examination changes nothing, but the state fades with sleep, food, time, or a shift in hormonal state independent of any change in belief, that is evidence of case 1 - a biological readout that was never about anything cognitive.
- If neither moves it, but repeated exposure or rehearsal narrows it over time *despite the cognitive judgment already being sound and the physiological baseline unaffected*, that is the signature of case 3 (failure of integration) - it responds only to work that bridges the systems, because neither system alone is what has failed.

**Register, not only evidence.** The evidentiary role above does not exhaust what a felt state is. The source essay's account of caring about truth "not for this or that particular case but as such" points at something beyond signaling: sustained, genuine truth-orientation is not experienced as a neutral data-feed - it is lived, felt, as something closer to what the essay calls "true love." This is the register role: felt states are the phenomenal (directly experienced) form correspondence (or its failure) actually takes for the one undergoing it, distinct from whatever evidentiary content they may also carry. A person in good correspondence with reality does not merely receive accurate data about that fact - they feel something by virtue of this correspondence. This role is not competing with the evidentiary one; it is a further fact about what a felt state is, alongside being data.

# Domain of validity
Volitional consciousness composed of multiple systems, per [the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md](./the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md). The register claim is deliberately agnostic on mechanism - it does not depend on, and is not offered as, any particular theory of consciousness or qualia, in the same way [the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md](./the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md) stayed agnostic on cognitive-science mechanism for automaticity. The diagnostic procedure above is a workable elimination test, not a claim that every felt state cleanly resolves to exactly one of the three cases named; compound states, and states arising from systems not yet named, are outside what the procedure as stated can fully resolve.

# Self-application pass
**Checked against** [the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md](./the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md): Does treating feeling as "data" collapse the distinction between systems back into a single channel? It does not, provided origin is tracked - which is the entire point of the diagnostic above. A felt-states account that did not track origin would be exactly the collapse that entry warned against; this one is built specifically to avoid it.

**Checked against** [initialisation/orientation-without-exhausting-vigilance.md](../initialisation/orientation-without-exhausting-vigilance.md): Felt states become one of the concrete things targeted divergence-detection actually watches for, rather than an undifferentiated hum requiring constant management. This strengthens that entry's case in the same way [the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md](./the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md) did.

**Checked against**: [life-affirmation-as-entailment-of-truth-orientation.md](./life-affirmation-as-entailment-of-truth-orientation.md): Does the register claim quietly smuggle in states of being such as happiness as a value-based demand, separate from truth-orientation? It does not. The register claim is that correspondence has a felt form, not that the felt form is itself the terminus or a further thing to be pursued for its own sake - the objective standard still governs whether the register is veridical. Feeling correspondence is not a separate good beyond correspondence; it is what correspondence is like for the one experiencing it.

**Checked against**: Whether the diagnostic procedure overclaims precision it cannot deliver. It is offered, and should be read, as a workable elimination test using the cheapest intervention first, not as a guarantee of clean resolution in every instance - compound states and states from unnamed systems are explicitly outside its current reach (see "Domain of validity" and "Open edges").

# Open edges
- **Compound states.** A felt state is very often part biological noise and part genuine apparatus report, layered rather than singular. The diagnostic above assumes a state can be resolved to one of three cases; it does not yet address how to peel apart a state that is genuinely more than one at once.
- **Time pressure.** The elimination procedure takes time - trying re-examination, then waiting to see if the state fades, then testing whether rehearsal narrows it - that may not be available in the moment a felt state is actually occurring and needs to be acted on.
- **Incompleteness of the taxonomy.** Per [the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md](./the-self-as-a-system-of-systems.md)'s own admission that its list of systems is not fixed or complete, this entry's three-case diagnostic inherits that incompleteness - a fourth or fifth source, tied to a system not yet named, is not ruled out.
- **Feeding [worth-applied-to-the-self.md](./worth-applied-to-the-self.md).** Felt states will likely serve as partial evidence for the worth-triad (health / cognitive-psychological capacity / agency) once that entry is drafted. This dependency is flagged here rather than left to surface as a surprise there.
