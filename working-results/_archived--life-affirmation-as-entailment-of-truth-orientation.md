**IV. Terminus**

<h1>Life-Affirmation as Entailment of Truth-Orientation</h1>

> **Preliminary form**: ["The Purpose of Life", library/lifeblood-essays.odt](../library/lifeblood-essays.odt) ("source essay")

> **Key reference**: ["Standard of Value" / "Nature of Life", Ethics, Philosophy, mistermilvusmigrans.github.io](https://mistermilvusmigrans.github.io/philosophy/ethics/1-standard-of-value.html) (*second derivation route*)

--

# Statement
Life-affirmation is not a second terminus standing alongside correspondence (the axiom of choice). It is the first-order entailment of taking the axiom seriously as a *continual* orientation rather than an episodic one. There is only one terminus in this system - correspondence - and life-affirmation falls out of what "continual" correspondence means once you refuse to flatten it into a series of disconnected, one-off acts of accuracy.

# Derivation
Start from the axiom as already stated in [foundation/system-foundation.md](../foundation/system-foundation.md#the-axiom-of-choice-the-one-axiom-this-system-adds): *consciousness ought to bring itself into correspondence with what is.* Notice the verb form already carries continuity in it - "ought to bring itself" describes a standing orientation, not a single successful check against reality performed once and then set down.

Caring about truth *episodically* - getting one fact right, then moving on without further commitment - is nearly trivial and does not require anything further of the one doing the caring. But caring about truth *as such*, "not for this or that particular case," is a different and much heavier commitment: it is a commitment to an ongoing process of engagement. And an ongoing process analytically requires something that persists to keep carrying it out. This is not a second value being smuggled in beside truth-orientation - it is simply what "continual" means, unpacked. For the engagement to continue, the engager must continue. Sustaining the engager, i.e. life-affirmation, is therefore not chosen for its own sake as an independent terminus; it is required *because* truth-orientation, taken seriously as continual rather than episodic, cannot coherently be separated from the sustained existence of the one doing the orienting.

This gives the corrected structure:

```
axiom of choice (correspondence)
      -> taken seriously as continual, not episodic
      -> entails sustaining the one who corresponds
      -> life-affirmation (derived, not axiomatic)
```

**A second, prior derivation route.** The continuity argument above derives life-sustenance *from* the demand that truth-orientation be ongoing rather than one-off. "Standard of Value" reaches the same conclusion by a more fundamental route, one that does not depend on continuity as a premise at all: it examines what a *goal* is, as such. A goal is self-generated action consistently oriented toward a potential, and a self-sustaining goal - one whose achievement is a necessary condition for further pursuing it - is what life *is*. The essay then shows that an indestructible, non-conditional being could not coherently have goals about its own existence at all, since nothing about that existence is ever at stake; anything it "pursues" would have to reference something outside itself, making it, in the essay's own terms, "essentially an automaton, goal-driven only in a derivative sense." Truth-orientation is itself a goal. So this route does not need to first establish that truth-orientation must be continual before it can require a persisting engager - it shows that goal-directed truth-orientation could not be a coherent goal *at all*, for *any* entity, absent the conditionality that life is. This is logically prior to the continuity argument: the continuity argument explains why an ongoing pursuit needs a persisting entity; this route explains why "pursuit" - continual or not - presupposes a conditional, living entity in the first place. The two derivations converge on the same entailment from different starting points, which is the correct relationship for them to have to each other, rather than one simply restating the other.

> **A note on the weight of this convergence**: The source essay above and the "Standard of Value" / "Nature of Life" essays appear to originate from the same authorial project this repository itself is named after (mistermilvusmigrans). This is flagged rather than smoothed over, because it means the two derivation routes should not be treated as fully independent corroboration in the way, e.g., two unrelated philosophical traditions arriving at the same conclusion would be. What can be claimed honestly is that the two routes are *logically* independent of each other (neither presupposes the other), even if they are not *biographically* independent. The self-application pass below treats the convergence accordingly - as structural support, not as two separate witnesses.

Two further pieces from the source essay confirm this:

**The self-validation is the same proof pattern used for the axiom itself.** [foundation/system-foundation.md](../foundation/system-foundation.md#the-axiom-of-choice-the-one-axiom-this-system-adds) already establishes that the axiom is not quite forced and not quite arbitrary, because the moment anyone stops to ask whether they should care about correspondence, they are already exercising it - the deliberation borrows legitimacy from the very standard it would need to argue against. The source essay runs the identical move one level down, specifically for the choice to care about existence: *"why prefer non-arbitrary choices? ... if you are asking about purpose, seeking reasons, engaging in philosophical inquiry, you are already demonstrating a preference for non-arbitrary thinking. The question answers itself through its asking."* This is not a separate justification technique invented for life-affirmation, but rather, it is the axiom's own self-validating structure, inherited by its entailment, which is exactly what should happen if the derivation above is correct rather than merely plausible-sounding.

**The reason offered is explicitly not a value-based demand.** The essay is careful on this point: *"why should you care about your life? Because it affirms truth, but that is a valid reason rather than a value-based demand."* This distinction is load-bearing for keeping the system at one axiom. A value-based demand ("life is intrinsically valuable, full stop") would be a second, independently-justified terminus and would reopen exactly the problem this entry exists to close. What is being claimed instead is narrower and structural: given that truth-orientation is continual, life-sustenance is what continuity requires, not something chosen for its own separate sake. The essay's own gloss agrees: *"Life does not have a purpose. Life is a purpose, and being a self-generated, self-sustaining act, it is a self-fulfilling purpose."* Life is not gaining a purpose from outside itself; it *is* the enacted form of continual correspondence.

# Domain of validity
This holds for any consciousness capable of continual (as opposed to only episodic) truth-orientation - i.e. a volitional, conceptual consciousness of the kind already described in [action-as-total-expression.md](./action-as-total-expression.md) and its source material on the self as an integrated feedback loop. The source essay itself flags the boundary case explicitly: when life "as the kind of being we are - volitional beings - becomes impossible due to the destruction of potential (e.g. through a degenerative and/or terminal illness, through physical/practical incapacitation, etc.)," the entailment's grip may not hold the same way, since the thing being sustained (a volitional engager capable of continual correspondence) is what has been destroyed. This entry assumes that continuity remains possible in principle; it does not cover that boundary and should not be read as silently extending into it.

# Self-application pass
**Checked against**: Whether this entry smuggles in an unearned value premise, which is the exact failure mode it was built to close off. It does not, on the following ground: the claim is not "life is valuable" (a value claim requiring its own justification, which would reintroduce a second terminus) but "sustaining the engager is what continual truth-orientation requires" (a structural claim about what continuity means, requiring no further justification beyond the axiom already adopted). The source essay's own distinction between a "valid reason" and a "value-based demand" is doing real work here and was checked directly against the derivation above rather than taken on faith.

**Checked against**: Whether the second derivation route is being overclaimed as independent corroboration when it may not be biographically independent (see note above). Flagged rather than resolved: the two routes remain logically independent of each other, which is the property that actually matters for whether the merge strengthens the entry.

**Checked against**: whether this quietly reopens the descriptive/prescriptive gap already flagged as unresolved in [leverage-governs-allocation.md](./leverage-governs-allocation.md). It does not resolve that gap and does not claim to; the two entries are independent.

# Open edges
- **Dominance, not just requirement.** This entry establishes that life-affirmation is *required* once truth-orientation is taken as continual. It does not establish *how strongly* this floor dominates other derived values in the hierarchy - whether sustaining the engager simply floors every leverage calculation beneath it, or can be locally outweighed in specific derived cases the way [cruelty-licensed-only-as-cost-of-contact.md](./cruelty-licensed-only-as-cost-of-contact.md) allows incidental costs to be licensed. This needs its own working-result before it can be relied on in edge cases.
- **The boundary case flagged in "Domain of validity"** (destruction of the volitional potential that continual correspondence requires) is named but not worked through here. Whether the entailment simply stops applying at that boundary, or degrades gracefully, or hands off to a different principle entirely, is open.
- **Which "life" is entailed.** The source essay is specific that what is at stake is "life as the kind of being we are - volitional beings," not bare biological continuation. This entry inherits that narrower reading. If it is later used to justify claims about biological survival as such, that would be a silent widening of scope and should be caught, not assumed.
