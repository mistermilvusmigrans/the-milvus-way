# Statement
A premise's mere presence in the apparatus, however it was installed, is not itself a fault - [the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md](./the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md) already establishes that the self, at any moment, simply is its current configuration, unexamined content included. Responsibility attaches only to leaving a premise unexamined *when genuine occasion to examine it has arisen* - and, per that entry's process-validation refinement, "examine" means being able to answer for the premise's history (or its process's history), not re-deriving it from first principles each time.

# Derivation
This entry does not reopen whether unexamined content is itself a fault - [the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md](./the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md) already closed that question. What remains is narrower and was deliberately left open there: what is actually owed, once an unexamined premise is in play.

**Defining "genuine occasion," rather than leaving it to intuition.** An occasion is genuine, and therefore triggers responsibility, when something non-arbitrary brings the premise into relevant range: a felt divergence (of the kind [felt-states-as-data-and-register.md](./felt-states-as-data-and-register.md) shows can be diagnosed rather than merely noticed), a decision that rests on the premise or a consequence surfacing that traces back to it. This is not a new invention for this entry - it is "Principles for Rationality in Practice"'s essentials-first and principle of unaccountability, applied to the self's own internal content rather than to external facts: what cannot be taken into account (an arbitrary worry that no divergence, decision or consequence has actually raised) must not be taken into account, and this is exactly what forecloses the exhausting-vigilance failure mode [initialisation/orientation-without-exhausting-vigilance.md](../initialisation/orientation-without-exhausting-vigilance.md) already warns against. A person is not responsible for constantly auditing every premise they hold; they are responsible for not looking away once one has been genuinely raised.

**Where genuine occasion actually comes from, made concrete.** [felt-states-as-data-and-register.md](./felt-states-as-data-and-register.md) gives this entry a working account of how an occasion announces itself in the first place. An apparatus-level divergence report (case 2 ("apparatus-level divergence report") in that entry's diagnostic) is often exactly the mechanism by which a genuine occasion to examine a premise first becomes available at all. This means this entry's account of occasion is not independent of [felt-states-as-data-and-register.md](./felt-states-as-data-and-register.md)'s account of felt states - it depends on it directly, and should be read as depending on it rather than as a coincidental overlap.

**The hard case: evasion, not innocence.** The account so far covers the innocent case - no occasion has yet arisen, so there is nothing yet to answer for. The harder case is content avoided specifically *because* examining it would cost something: active evasion rather than mere non-occurrence of an occasion. This case has its own detectable signature, already named in [initialisation/orientation-without-exhausting-vigilance.md](../initialisation/orientation-without-exhausting-vigilance.md): the difference between defending a conclusion and testing it, between avoiding a piece of information and seeking it. Detecting that signature in oneself is itself evidence that an occasion was already noticed and then suppressed, which converts what would otherwise be described as "no occasion arose" into "an occasion arose and was refused." This is the actual fault case this entry is built to name: not the presence of an unexamined premise, and not even the passage of time without examining it, but the detectable move of turning away from a premise once its relevance has already registered.

# Domain of validity
Scoped as [the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md](./the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md) is scoped - the cognitive/psychological system specifically, not the whole self. This entry does not extend responsibility to biological-system automaticity; hence, for example, a hormonal reaction is not something one is responsible for "examining" in the sense this entry describes, and nothing here should be read as implying otherwise.

# Self-application pass
**Checked against** [the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md](./the-subconscious-as-apparatus.md): Does assigning fault here quietly reopen blame for biological automaticity, given how easily "examine your premises" language can slide into "examine your reactions" generally? It does not, and the restriction is stated explicitly above.

**Checked against** [initialisation/the-baseline-obligation.md](../initialisation/the-baseline-obligation.md): This entry is arguably that obligation's operational form, applied specifically to self-directed ignorance rather than ignorance of the world or of other minds. The baseline obligation names contact with "the edges of one's own ignorance" as owed; this entry specifies what discharging that owed contact actually requires and what triggers it, for premises about oneself specifically.

**Checked against** [initialisation/orientation-without-exhausting-vigilance.md](../initialisation/orientation-without-exhausting-vigilance.md): The evasion-signature move is reused wholesale from that entry, not reinvented for this one. Checked directly: the signature in question there (defending vs. testing a conclusion, avoiding vs. seeking information) is the same signature being invoked here, applied to the self's own held premises rather than to external claims.

**Checked against**: Overclaiming resolution of the regress named in "Open edges": this entry does not claim the evasion-detection process is itself immune to the evasion it detects; that limitation is named directly rather than argued away.

# Open edges
- **A regress worry.** If evasion is detected via a signature, and evasion is partly defined as suppressing detection, what stops evasion from also suppressing the signature-detection process itself? This is named rather than resolved. It may turn out that this is simply a limit on what any self-monitoring process can guarantee from the inside, rather than a flaw specific to this account - but that has not been established here.
- **Where the line for "genuine occasion" actually sits in hard cases.** The definition given (a felt divergence, a resting decision or a surfacing consequence) is workable for clear cases but has not been tested against ambiguous ones - for instance, a slow-building pattern of small consequences, none individually enough to constitute an occasion, that only becomes a genuine occasion in aggregate. This remains the sharpest unresolved point in the entry.
- **Dependency on [felt-states-as-data-and-register.md](./felt-states-as-data-and-register.md)'s diagnostic.** Since this entry's account of occasion leans on [felt-states-as-data-and-register.md](./felt-states-as-data-and-register.md)'s diagnostic for telling divergence-reports apart from other felt states, any future revision to that diagnostic (particularly its open edges on compound states) will need to be checked against this entry's account of occasion as well.
