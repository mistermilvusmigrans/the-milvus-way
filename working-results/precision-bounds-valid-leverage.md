**III. Allocation**

<h1>Precision Bounds Valid Leverage</h1>

> **Preliminary form**: ["Precision", `library/lifeblood-essays.odt`](../library/lifeblood-essays.odt)

---

# Statement
Precision and leverage are not opposing pulls to be traded off against each other. Precision, correctly defined as fit to a given context and purpose rather than as the inverse of abstraction, is the validity-condition a compression must satisfy to count as leverage at all. A compression that has dropped implications relevant to its declared purpose is not a more-leveraged, less-precise result; it is not leverage, it is noise that has borrowed leverage's shape.

# Derivation
[leverage-governs-allocation.md](./leverage-governs-allocation.md) establishes that correspondence ought to be pursued in proportion to how much further correspondence a given piece of contact unlocks, rather than in proportion to sheer volume. Read naively, this can seem to pull against precision, since maximal compression looks like it wants looser, broader units, while precision looks like it wants tighter, narrower ones.

This apparent opposition rests on a mistaken model of precision as an absolute scale running from "vague" to "exact," with abstraction pushing toward the vague end. The source essay defines precision differently: *"the description of the limits of the potential implications of one or more units for the given context and purpose."* Precision is always relative to context and purpose, not an absolute property of a unit by itself - indeed, the source essay is explicit that the right question is not "how much precision" but "how much precision about what," and states directly that *"the level of abstraction is not inversely related to the level of precision."* "Tall" is imprecise for engineering and perfectly precise for casual description of a person; a highly abstract principle can be exactly as precise as it needs to be for its purpose while a narrow, concrete-bound claim can be sloppy for its own.

Given this, the actual mechanism connecting the two concepts is integration. Leverage is achieved through compression, and compression, cognitively, *is* integration - taking multiple units and forming a higher-order unit that stands in for them. The source essay's second mechanism of precision states this dependency directly: *"integration requires matching levels of abstraction and the corresponding kinds of precision across different domains. If your physics is concrete and your metaphysics is foggy, they cannot be coherently integrated."* An integration built on imprecise inputs does not produce a valid higher-level compression; it produces something that has the outward shape of compression (fewer units, broader claim) while having silently lost implications the declared purpose actually needed. This is indistinguishable, from the outside, from valid leverage, until the dropped implication turns out to matter, at which point the "compression" fails exactly where real leverage would have held.

This yields a single, non-competing criterion, replacing the naive trade-off: **a compression counts as leverage only if it is maximally precise for its declared context and purpose, while being maximally general across the range of contexts where that purpose recurs.** Precision does not compete with generality here; it is what the generality is allowed to rest on.

# Domain of validity
General, in the same sense [leverage-governs-allocation.md](./leverage-governs-allocation.md) is general - this is a validity-condition on that entry's own central concept, not a case-bound result tested against a single named scenario.

# Self-application pass
**Checked against**: The open edge already recorded in [leverage-governs-allocation.md](./leverage-governs-allocation.md), which states that "leverage" smuggles in a judgement of what is "important," resting on the concept of essentials, which are themselves context-and-purpose dependent, and flags this as asserted rather than derived. The current entry introduces precision as a means to help sharpen what constitutes context and purpose.

**Checked against**: Whether this entry itself commits the error it describes, i.e. whether it has compressed "precision" and "leverage" together in a way that drops a relevant implication of either concept. The fourth mechanism of the essay ["Precision" (`library/lifeblood-essays.odt`)](../library/lifeblood-essays.odt) ("precision is cognitively liberating... by constraining the boundaries of concepts, precision actually frees thought") was checked against the statement above and is consistent with it: the criterion given does not ask for more precision everywhere, only precision matched to declared purpose, which is the liberating version, not the restrictive one.

# Open edges
- **No operational test is given.** The criterion states what a compression must satisfy to count as leverage, but not how to check, before acting on a compression, whether it has silently dropped a purpose-relevant implication. This entry names the standard; it does not supply the check.
- **Interaction with the terminus entry.** [life-affirmation-as-entailment-of-truth-orientation.md](./life-affirmation-as-entailment-of-truth-orientation.md) establishes life-sustenance as a floor beneath the system. Whether "purpose," for the purposes of this entry's criterion, must always ultimately be checked against that floor, or whether it can be locally scoped without doing so on every leverage calculation, is not resolved here and would need its own working-result if it turns out to matter in practice.
