# Statement
Life-affirmation (as well as its species, truth-orientation) is fundamentally about engaging with reality directly, i.e. via experience. Everything else - from principles to purposes - is in service of this engagement, not the other way around. Hence, life-affirmation - considering life as a whole - is not an optimisation problem, i.e. not a question of how to "maximise" engagement, self-sustenance, or any such metric.

# Derivation
**Part 1: The nature of self-preservation**:

Self-preservation means the preservation of one's own existence as a volitional being. What "life" means in the context of a volitional being is a self-driven (i.e. volitional) survival. Hence, as volitional beings, our survival depends on not only us being able to sustain our lives but also on us seeing our lives as worth sustaining. "Worth" in this context is our evaluation of the potential of our existence, worked out in full in [worth-applied-to-the-self.md](./worth-applied-to-the-self.md).

> **Reference**: "Self-preservation for a volitional being" from "The Nature of Life"

**A flag, not yet resolved**: [worth-applied-to-the-self.md](./worth-applied-to-the-self.md) states its own standard in explicitly comparative terms - "the more the potential for each, the more the worth" - which reads as maximising language of exactly the kind this entry's "Statement" sets itself against. The "Domain of validity" section below addresses this at the general level (sub-level pursuits may validly be optimisation problems in service of life as a whole), but this specific tension with worth's own formulation has not been checked directly and is left here as a named gap rather than smoothed over.

---

**Part 2: The nature of death**:

Death as such has no lived-reality referent. What a volition being can experience is not death itself but the degradation or destruction of its life's potential. Hence, as a volitional being, it is not the end of one's life to which one can tie one's interests but rather one's experience of life and one's efficacy in sustaining it as one lives it. Death as such is a logical corollary of life's conditional nature, but, in and of itself, it is not something that can be treated as a part of one's pursuit of life.

> **Reference**: "Self-preservation for a volitional being" from "The Nature of Life"

**A flag, not yet resolved**: [life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md](./life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md) leaves open how to tell whether an action risking continued existence for the sake of correspondence is life-affirmation carried to its conclusion or self-undermining failure wearing that justification. This claim - that death as such has no lived-reality referent, and cannot itself be the thing one's interest is tied to - bears directly on that open question. Whether it sharpens the open edge, dissolves it, or complicates it further has not been worked out, and is named here rather than pursued.

---

**Part 3: Truth is ultimately concrete/particular**:

Metaphysically, only particulars exist. Abstractions are epistemological units, not metaphysical ones, and by their nature, abstractions refer to concretes and are reducible to concretes. Hence, truth-orientation is ultimately about the concrete facts of existence, which, ultimately, consists of direct, first-person experience. Now, since life-affirmation is the genus of truth-orientation (see: [life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md](./life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md)), truth-orientation is ultimately about the concrete engagement with existence, i.e. it consists of direct, first-person experience with the purpose of sustaining it.

---

**Synthesis**:

Hence, it must be appreciated that the pursuit to sustain of one's existence must be aligned with the fact that concrete engagement with existence is as much the fulfillment of one's pursuits as it is their fuel. To fixate on self-sustenance without engaging with one's existence concretely, i.e. in the moment, as it actually is, is a failure to value truth-orientation and life-affirmation. As parts 1 and 2 have shown, self-preservation is not fundamentally about longevity, and part 3 shows life-affirmation is fundamentally about concrete engagement, which means everything else - from improving health/fitness to seeking knowledge - is in service of it and cannot be done in a way that degrades/deteriorates the appreciation of concrete engagement.

# Domain of validity
Applies for life-affirmation for a volitional being. Specifically, it applies for life as such, i.e. as a whole, rather than specific purposes or pursuits. Certain purposes or pursuits may well be optimisation problems and validly so, but these must be in service of life as a whole, as described in "Derivation", not optimisation done for optimisation's sake.

# Self-application pass
**Checked against**: Whether it redefines truth-orientation or the axiom of choice in a way that violates the original intent. It does not, since these terms were defined in a way that left open the question of what exactly "life" and "truth" mean. Both these concepts have been intensively explored in "The Nature of Life" and in my own writings on epistemology (see: [**Epistemology**, **Philosophy**, mistermilvusmigrans.github.io](https://mistermilvusmigrans.github.io/philosophy/epistemology/)), and "Derivation" aligns with the positions of these writings.

**Checked against**: Whether it draws more from what "feels" good to live by, rather than objective facts about life and truth-seeking. This distinction is irrelevant to the validity of the arguments, since none of the arguments rely on any appeals to such a feeling. Moreover, if direct experience is fundamental to truth, the experience of what feels good in the long-term, sustainably, is not epistemologically inferior to the facts about life and truth-seeking; the former serves as a signal that prompts us to consider or reconsider the frame set by the latter. This should be read alongside, not in place of, [felt-states-as-data-and-register.md](./felt-states-as-data-and-register.md), which keeps a felt state's evidentiary role (data about worth) distinct from its register role (the felt form correspondence takes) and holds that the objective standard still governs whether a given register is veridical. The claim made here is not that feeling settles what is true, only that it is not a lesser-order signal than propositional fact - this has not been fully checked against that entry's diagnostic apparatus and is flagged rather than resolved.

**Checked against**: Whether "Derivation"'s part 3's premise - that only particulars exist, and abstractions are epistemological rather than metaphysical units - is a new foundational commitment being imported without being checked against [foundation/system-foundation.md](../foundation/system-foundation.md), which otherwise imports its external metaphysics "wholesale" and explicitly as a bounded, named import. It is not a new foundational commitment, and is largely implicit in the metaphysics and explicitly indicated in this line from ["Axiom 2: The law of identity", **Metaphysics**, **Philosophy**, mistermilvusmigrans.github.io](https://mistermilvusmigrans.github.io/philosophy/metaphysics/essentials-of-metaphysics.html):

> When I say “thing”, I am referring to a particular, concrete part of reality, which can be identified through a variety of abstractions that focus on some attributes and omit the rest.

**Checked against**: Whether it smuggles a prioritisation of the present over the potential, despite the life of a volitional being described as one that integrates the two (see: [life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md](./life-affirmation-and-truth-orientation.md)). It does not. The pursuit of potential is based on what is actually - currently - recognised and pursued (either actively or indirectly, via attention/alertness for some signal or condition regarding its pursuit/achievement). One's concrete engagement with existence is about engaging with existence as it is, but this does not preclude purpose that aims to shape what is to what can be even as appreciating what is.

# Open edges
- **Can some form of "optimisation" be valid for life as a whole?** While this working-result denies that life-affirmation is fundamentally an optimisation problem by affirming what it fundamentally is, it is uncertain whether optimisation, if properly defined and scoped, could be applicable for some aspect of life-affirmation fundamentally, because "optimisation" as a term depends entirely on context and defined goals/standards.
- **What are the practical implications of this view?** This is admittedly unclear at this stage, and I aim to flesh this out with subsequent working results and practices.
- **The "Statement" risks overclaiming relative to its own "Domain of validity".** "Life-affirmation... is not an optimisation problem" is stated flatly in the Statement, but "Domain of validity" immediately qualifies this - "certain purposes or pursuits may well be optimisation problems and validly so... in service of life as a whole." The two are compatible on a careful reading, but the "Statement" alone asserts more than the entry actually defends, and should be read with that qualification in mind rather than at face value.
- **The jump from part 3 to the synthesis is under-argued in "Derivation".** Part 3 establishes a metaphysical claim - that abstractions refer to and are reducible to concretes. The synthesis then treats this as licensing an ethical claim - that concrete engagement is "as much the fulfillment... as it is the fuel," and that abstracted pursuit is secondary to it. The connective tissue between "abstractions are metaphysically reducible to concretes" and "concrete engagement is what pursuits are ultimately for" is not fully supplied; the two claims currently share the word "concrete" without an argument establishing that the metaphysical sense and the lived/ethical sense of the word carry the same weight.
