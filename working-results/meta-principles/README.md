# About
A generalisation is the integration of one or more concepts with observations about their referents, an integration that applies not just for the particular context of the observations but for an *unlimited range* of contexts. In other words, a generalisation is the result of inductive reasoning, i.e. reasoning that moves from particular knowledge to universal knowledge/knowledge-claims. For example, the statement "increasing disposable income improves academic performance" is a generalisation (be it true or false) that relates disposable income (as a concept) to academic performance (as a concept) across a range of socio-economic contexts, based on particular observations/data points that (1) correlate the metrics associated with these two concepts, and (2) capture some sort of causal relationship (e.g. more disposable income directly leads to easier and more reliable access to study-resources, more disposable income indirectly leads to less stress about non-study-related areas, thereby freeing focus, etc.).

A principle is a generalisation of generalisations, i.e. a kind of generalisation that integrates two or more specific generalisations in some respect(s), such that the specific generalisations involve it and are context-specific instantiations of it. For example, the statement "human well-being is dependent on economic conditions" is a principle that generalises over specific generalisations such as "increasing disposable income improves academic performance", "improving finances improves mobility, which improves access to socio-economic opportunities", "economic growth lead to more job opportunities". Note that here, I aim to clarify my concepts, and whether or to what extent these generalisations are true is out of scope here.

Meta-principles are principles about principles + generalisations, i.e. a kind of principle that integrates two or more principles + generalisations in some respect(s), such that the specific principles involve it and are context-specific instantiations of it. For example, the statement "material conditions are always operative for human well-being, to some extent" is a meta-principle that underlies the approach taken in the principles "human well-being is dependent on economic conditions", "environments have both physical and psychological impacts on humans", etc. The meta-principle grounds the principles in a broader approach and/or truth, and thus, allows us to (1) organise specific principles more logically, and (2) generate further principles of a similar kind in different contexts.

> **NOTE**:
> 
> - "Generalisation" is the broadest concept <br> => *A principle and a meta-principle are both generalisations*
> - "Principle" is the second-broadest concept <br> => *A meta-principle is a principle*
