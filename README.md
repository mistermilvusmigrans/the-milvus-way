## Welcome
There is a kind of mathematician that pursues excellence not for the sake of excellence, but for the truth it lets them reach. Excellence, to them, is instrumental. This is what morality is to me. Moral goodness is not the goal, contact and correspondence with reality is; morality, such as it is, is a means to this end and nothing more. Moral judgement, in the end, asks only one question: am I making the world mine, not possessively, but greedily, graciously? This system is structured as a codebase, not as a treatise, where I do not need everything settled, but I need to know what is and is not settled, and keep this map up-to-date. This is, after all, the way of the cartographer, a cartographer who is part of the terrain they are in love with.
