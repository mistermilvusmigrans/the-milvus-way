# About
Many months ago from today (2026-07), I had created a character in a story of 2 sisters. It was the story of Maitreyi, the sister who sought to make the world hers, and the sister for whom the joyous existence of his little sister, Minerva, was a source of not just joy but... inspiration, devotional inspiration. Before I move on, let me lay out parts of my sketches for these characters.

# Character Sketches
**Maitreyi**:

A key philosophical reference for this character is Yajnavalkya and Maitreyee's dialogue from the Brihadaranyaka Upanishad. How does Yajnavalkya and Maitreyee's dialogue feed into my Maitreyi's character, through the lens of my perspective? Here is the key link: to live with one's full volition is to live for one's full desire for the potential, for potential is the motive-force of goal-driven beings, and for a being whose very self-aware consciousness is goal-driven - i.e. a volitional being - this motive-force becomes active, self-aware and all-consuming desire. Her whole approach is a constant wrestle between the impulse to explore her desires and the focus to integrate her actions toward the greatest fulfillment of these very desires. And what greater fulfillment is there than to grapple with your very world, intimately, intricately and intensely. This is why she "becomes an eye" when she sees the stars, this is the root of her "romance with the world", this is why thought of wrestling with the storm arouses her, and this is why she absolutely adores Minerva, the human being who she's partly raised, and wholly witnessed from a young age blossoming into someone who embodies her love of life as intensely as her, though in her own distinct way - this distinction is actually key, as it draws Maitreyi to Minerva even more, as Maitreyi's primary drive is to integrate, and what better way to integrate value than to engage deeply with its varied embodiments?

To my Maitreyi, to love her "self" is to love existence, not in the way of loving the "one indivisible consciousness", but in the way of integrating environment and entity through identity and causality; what is, is, and acts as per what it is, and acts and is acted upon accordingly... and she is, and to her, her "self" is the focal point of all that is. She loves existence as an environment and extension of her embodied reality, not as something metaphysically separate or as something existentially identical. She is the ancient Maitreyee, if the ancient Maitreyee were a devotee of sense-perception and reason. When her "self dissolves", she does not become "one" but claims the world for herself.

---

**Minerva**:

Named after the Etruscan/Roman goddess of wisdom, her character is defined by a wisdom that is far more embodied and engaged. She is the concretisation of the goal of Maitreyi's study: an integrated being who loves life. While Maitreyi expresses her love of life through all-consuming devotion, Minerva expresses it through all-embracing joy. Maitreyi's devotion becomes clearer with Minerva in the picture, and Minerva's joy becomes deeper and more grounded with Maitreyi in the picture. She does not split emotion and reason, she ensures emotion and reason serve her joy. She does not set the intellectual, the practical and the pleasurable into separate boxes, she makes sure they work together to... you guessed it, serve her joy. Her background reflects this approach. She began as a biologist, following her beloved sister's footsteps, but soon, via anatomy and a sheer aesthetic passion for the human form, she became a sculptor and a modeller.

# Synthesis
Here is a key line: Maitreyi's primary drive is to integrate. She is a lover of this world, in the embodied, intellectually/emotionally engaged sense of the word. Minerva is not her muse but the person who embodies her values in a way that concretises the hope and joy she herself may not always be in touch with. She describes herself as sort of a wanderer, wavering at times, but always seeking, integrating.

I describe her at such length because I realise that, through her, I helped personify a part of me I had never quite known what to do with. It is a part of me I had felt I had to keep aside most of the time, sideline it as it was "impractical" and "obsessive". But it was only with the creation of Maitreyi that I saw this side for the best it could be: a lover of truth, not dispassionately but in a way that was embodied, that loved and cherished joy and flourishing even when despair put a weight in its core.

In my moments of self-dejection, when I saw myself and saw little worth fighting for in the fog of despair, when I could perhaps respect or even admire parts of me but never enough to anchor me to the world, I saw the Maitreyi in me and realised that I loved her. Desperately. Defiantly. She made my existence worth fighting for, because she fought for it with her every breath, devoted, warm, wandering, sometimes wavering, but never letting go, her eyes in love that they could see.

I love her. Thus, I love life.

As for Minerva, Maitreyi loves Minerva, and that is enough for me.

# Love Poems
## Maitreyi to Minerva
### The World in Focus
The sight of you brings the world into sight,

Mud and mountains, sky and stone.

Began a wanderer, became an anchorite,

Bound in your being, together and alone.

### My Dark-Eyed One
Oh, dark-eyed one, in the sun, drinking

Every ray that reaches your eyes,

Nothing unseen, no gaze unthinking,

Yet your radiance do you realise?

...

Oh, one with lines of laughter

Fanning from apertures of love,

Cherish this world, now and hereafter,

My radiant kitten, my little dove.

## Minerva to Maitreyi
I look at you looking at me, your

Eyes in love that they could see.

I open my eyes, which are in love

That they had seen, seen you be.
