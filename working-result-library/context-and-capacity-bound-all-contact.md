# Statement
Full correspondence with reality was never meant globally, since no consciousness is in full contact with everything in every situation. Contact is always bounded by the particular context a person is in and the particular capacities they currently have. This is not a weakness in the framework; it is what keeps the framework honest about what a finite consciousness can do.

# Derivation
Follows from the bedrock fact that any entity, including a conscious one, has a particular identity and acts within a particular context; it cannot act outside what its identity and situation permit (see: ["The second cause: being and action", initialisation/the-foundational-conviction-stated.md](../initialisation/the-foundational-conviction-stated.md#the-second-clause-being-and-action)). Combined with the axiom of choice (namely that correspondence with reality is worth pursuing; see: ["The axiom of choice: the one axiom this system adds", foundation/system-foundation.md](../foundation/system-foundation.md#the-axiom-of-choice-the-one-axiom-this-system-adds")), this gives: pursue correspondence with reality as far as your actual context and capacity allow, not further, and do not treat the limit as failure by itself.

# Domain of validity
Specifically tested against the question of whether psychopathy is a counterexample to the framework, a case of intact factual modeling of another person with reduced or absent affective uptake of that fact. The current conclusion is that it is not fatal to the framework, because it is read as a case of bounded capacity (a particular kind of contact, with another mind's interiority specifically, not available to that consciousness) rather than evidence that full contact and moral failure can coexist.
