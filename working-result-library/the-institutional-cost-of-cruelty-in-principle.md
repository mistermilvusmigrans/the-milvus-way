# Statement
Sapient minds are not just individually valuable as high-leverage nodes of contact - they are collectively the substrate through which contact compounds across time, through language, inherited method, and institutions that let one mind's correspondence become available to others instead of dying with it. Permitting cruelty toward collaborators *in principle* - as opposed to as a contained, isolated act - degrades the general willingness of any mind to make itself legible to any other mind, since legibility now carries a standing risk.

This converts the dyadic "tax" established by [the-reciprocity-constraint.md](./the-reciprocity-constraint.md) into something close to a near-prohibition at the level of precedent: in this context, the single-instance license (see: [cruelty-licensed-only-as-cost-of-contact.md](./cruelty-licensed-only-as-cost-of-contact.md)), survives only as an abstract limit case, valid the way a frictionless plane is valid, because almost no real instance is actually isolated - asking "is this allowed in principle" has already imported the institutional question.

# Derivation
Built on [leverage-governs-allocation.md](./leverage-governs-allocation.md) applied at the level of collaborative networks rather than individual acts, combined with the reciprocity constraint above.

# Domain of validity
Stated and tested at the level of principle and precedent specifically - explicitly does not override the narrower, contained single-instance license established in [cruelty-licensed-only-as-cost-of-contact.md](./cruelty-licensed-only-as-cost-of-contact.md), which remains a true (if rare) limit case.

# Open edges
Has not yet been tested against cases where the "institution" in question is itself corrupt or actively opposed to correspondence (e.g. does the same argument protect trust within an institution that exists to produce systematic distortion). Worth a dedicated pass.
