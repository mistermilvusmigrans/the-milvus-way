# The difficulty: does generality outrank intimacy
The hierarchy of knowledge-seeking seems to privilege the general over the particular, the physicist over the glassblower. But this looks clearly wrong: a craftsman with extraordinary intimate knowledge of their material is not thereby in less contact with reality than someone who can derive that material's properties from equations.

# Resolution: leverage from where me stand
> **KEY IDEA**: The leverage hierarchy is:
> 
> - A property of knowledge structures in the abstract
> - Not a prescription for every individual

The right question for any person is not...

*"what has the most epistemic leverage in general?"*

... but:

> What has the most epistemic leverage from where I am standing?

# Three ways context determines leverage
- **Embeddedness**: Where I am embedded determines what is generative *for me*. Lived, embodied knowledge tested against resistance may have greater *fidelity* than general equations, which are broader but can be thinner, with more lost in translation between model and reality.
- **Social/collaborative structure**: Knowledge is distributed across a collective enterprise (e.g. bridge-building). Not everyone needs to understand physics if the system as a whole distributes that knowledge appropriately. An individual's epistemic role is partly determined by what position they occupy in a larger knowing system. This means deep specialism can be entirely right if it genuinely fills a gap in the broader map
- **Path dependency**: Me can only build on what me already have. For some, going deeper into what they partially know is more leveraged than pivoting to something more general. Genuine depth in one domain often reveals general principles from the inside. This means one may arrive at physics by becoming a serious glassblower, not by abandoning glass for textbooks

# The constant criterion: alive vs. inert knowledge
The hierarchy is real.

BUT:

The individual path through it is not fixed.

---

What stays constant is the criterion:

> Is my knowledge *alive*? I.e.: In my context, is it:
> 
> - Tested?
> - Connected?
> - Generative?
>
> Or is it *inert*? I.e.: In my context, are they:
> 
> - Facts held without genuine comprehension?
> - Abstractions ungrounded in experience?
> - Expertise that has become ritual rather than understanding?

---

*The enemy is not specialism or generalism.*

*It is knowledge that does not bite on reality.*
