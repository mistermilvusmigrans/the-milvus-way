# Key question
The path diverges legitimately above some point (see: [the-value-of-depth-reconciling-specialism.md](./the-value-of-depth-reconciling-specialism.md)). This implies a *baseline*, i.e. some contact with reality every person is obligated to maintain. Below that baseline, one is not merely ignorant; one is failing in a meaningful sense.

> What constitutes that baseline?

# The four irreducible domains

**1. The reality of my own nature**: I am finite: particular capacities, blind spots, desires that can deceive me, a body that situates me specifically in the world. Not knowing myself, honestly and unsentimentally, means the entire epistemic apparatus runs uncalibrated. Everything else runs through this, which means this is the most foundational obligation.

**2. The reality of other minds**: Other subjects have genuine interiority: their own contact with reality, suffering, stake in things. Being in contact with reality means this cannot be fully bracketed. Someone closed to others' interiority has a systematic blind spot, i.e. not just a moral failing, but an epistemic one: a vast domain of what exists goes missing.

**3. The reality of my immediate situation**: The actual conditions of my life, relationships, work, community. This is the slice of reality me most directly act upon and are acted upon by. Wilful ignorance here is perhaps the most common form of epistemic failure: not understanding the systems one is embedded in, the effects one has, the forces shaping one.

**4. The limits of my own knowing**: I do not need to know everything - but I need to know *that* I do not know, and roughly where my map goes blank. Without this: me cannot seek appropriately, and cannot collaborate with others whose maps cover different territory. This means this is the condition of all good epistemic conduct.

# Particularisation: role and capacity
The baseline particularises along 2 axes:

> - **Role**: <br> What slice of reality is relevant for me to act upon or within?
> - **Capacity**: <br> What I am actually able to come to know, given my starting point, time and makeup

The obligation:

- Go as deep and as wide as:
  - My role demands
  - My capacity allows
- While never abandoning the universal baseline

# Where the moral and the epistemic collapse into one
I posit that most of what is called moral failure maps onto failure at the baseline level. Cruelty, negligence, dishonesty, exploitation - these involve failures to be in genuine contact with the reality of other people, oneself or the actual consequences of one's actions.

---

*Moral and epistemic obligations collapse into one at the baseline.*

*They only legitimately diverge above it.*
