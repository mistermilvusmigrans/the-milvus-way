# The pushback
Sleep deprivation, hormonal states, emotional investment were earlier described as degradations to be managed. This is inconsistent with the no-mind-body-separation principle: each state offers its own epistemic texture.

> They do not just colour the signal but sometimes *are* the signal.

# Examples: sleep, hormones, emotion

**Sleep deprivation**: This impairs holding complexity, catching subtle errors, but also strips away excess consideration, focuses attention on what is most immediately real and urgent. Moreover, it gives direct information about itself - what it feels like, what it prioritises, what it reveals. Furthermore, if it emerged from genuine pursuit (a late night of real inquiry), it is not simply a cost; it is part of the texture of that pursuit.

**Hormonal states**: This may used to discount minds and reactions, being framed as distortion rather than perception. However, this framing gets it backwards: a phase that reduces tolerance for evasion and increases sensitivity to social cues is not malfunctioning but a different instrument picking up different frequencies. The question is not whether a state is ideal by a neutral standard, but what it is tracking, and whether that tracking is valid.

**Emotional investment** (love, fury, attraction): Love opens channels of perception that indifference and detachment keep closed. Investment is not contamination - it is fuel; it drives deeper testing and defense against better objections. Fury is not simply distortion: it has a focus, a target, an energy. The question is whether the focus is on the right thing, and the energy applied accurately.

# States require reading, not elimination
> - States are not straightforwardly okay
> - They are not obstacles to overcome
> - They are data that must be interpreted

This requires something that:

- Can move between states
- Without being dissolved by any of them

# The anchor: meta-awareness, not detachment
> **KEY IDEA**: The difference is between:
> 
> - *Being in* a state
> - *Being identified with* a state

This is not a neutral, stateless position - that doesn't exist. This is more like a meta-awareness that persists across states. Not detachment - detachment would lose the epistemic value of the states themselves - but the capacity to be fully in the wave while also knowing I am in a wave, i.e. letting each state do its epistemic work while retaining the thread back to the broader project.

# What the anchor is made of
- **A stable relationship to the question itself**: Orientation genuinely toward contact with reality (not toward being right, feeling good, or social approval) functions as a gyroscope - every state evaluated by: what is this showing me, and what is it obscuring?
- **Memory of one's own patterns**: Knowing how one specifically is distorted by specific states (e.g. anger narrows the causal field; attraction fills gaps charitably; exhaustion produces binary thinking), not as self-criticism, but as calibration, used to correct in real-time or flag outputs for later review.
- **Willingness to be shown wrong by reality**: This is the deepest component: an underlying commitment to let reality have the final word, against states and investments that pull toward certain conclusions.
- **Temporal extension**: A single state is always partial; the anchor is the capacity to extend one's view across time, so the late-night insight survives the morning, hormonal clarity is brought into dialogue with the baseline (see: [the-baseline-obligation.md](./the-baseline-obligation.md)), and chronic deprivation (losing its occasional epistemic value) is recognised as pure degradation.

    > *Time is the medium in which partial perspectives get integrated into something more whole.*
