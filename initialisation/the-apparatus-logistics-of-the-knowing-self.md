# Key question
The framework is self-generative. Thus, it must account for the material and psychological conditions of the knower. The knower is not a disembodied reasoner. Hence, the key question is: ***what are the actual logistics - mental, physical, intellectual, psychological, material, and what some call spiritual - of sustaining this project?***

# No ontological separation
> **KEY IDEA**:
> 
> - Mind and body, soul and matter, are not separate substances
> - They are one system seen from different angles

This means:

- Epistemic contact with reality is not upstream of physical state
- Rather, it *is* the physical state, among other things

# The body as instrument
Sleep, movement, nutrition, management of chronic stress are not preconditions satisfied before the real epistemic work begins. They are direct maintenance of the instrument. A chronically sleep-deprived person's capacity for accurate perception, holding complexity, and catching their own errors is genuinely degraded. They are in less contact with reality, not metaphorically but literally

> - The body is not the vessel of the mind
> - It is the apparatus by which the mind exists and acts

# Attention under siege
Attention is the core resource, and it is under siege. Contact with reality requires sustained, directed attention; attention has physical correlates, depletes, can be hijacked, is heavily shaped by environment

> Who and what controls my attention, and do I endorse that?

This implicates:

- Device/information-stream relationships
- Environment design
- The rhythm of one's days
- The people one spends time with

**NOTE**:

- These are not peripheral lifestyle questions
- These determine whether genuine epistemic contact is possible

# The psychological dimension
The barriers to contact with reality can be psychological:

- Fear of what one might find
- Investment in existing beliefs
- The social cost of seeing things others do not
- Shame that makes certain self-knowledge unbearable

These are not obstacles cleared before the epistemic project begins. They *are* the epistemic project, in large part. Working on psychological defences, attachment to comfortable distortions, capacity to tolerate uncertainty... this is not therapy as separate from philosophy. It is the same thing from a different angle.

# The social environment as error-correction
Other people are not merely objects in my reality. They are potential extensions of my knowing apparatus. Right relationships function like a distributed error-correction system: people who tell me when my model is wrong, who perceive what I do not, whose contact with reality is strong where mine is weak. This means the social environment is among the highest-leverage epistemic resources available. Here, it is key to note that certain social environments actively *degrade* epistemic contact, e.g. by rewarding performance over truth, punishing accurate perception, producing noise that drowns signal

# The spiritual dimension, reframed
If the no-separation principle is taken seriously, "spiritual practice" is the cultivation of a particular relationship to experience itself: openness, non-grasping, willingness to encounter what is rather than what is preferred. This is not mysticism grafted onto the framework. It is the psychological and attentional dimension taken to its logical depth. The capacity to be fully present to what is, without the constant mediation of anxiety or desire or narrative, is not a religious achievement. It is the apex of the epistemic project.*
